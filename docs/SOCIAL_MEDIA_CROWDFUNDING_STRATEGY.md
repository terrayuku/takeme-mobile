# TakeMe — Social Media, Crowdfunding & Marketing Strategy

---

## Core Narrative

Every piece of content, every campaign, every post ladders up to one sentence:

> **"Just raise your hand."**

That's the product. That's the story. A driverless taxi that sees you, stops for you, and takes you where you need to go — no app to open, no driver to convince, no cash to fumble with. The gesture is the interface. Lead with that everywhere.

---

## Brand Voice

- **Confident, not arrogant.** We're solving a real problem for real people.
- **Local first.** South African slang, references, and pride. This is built here, for here.
- **Human, not tech-bro.** The audience is taxi commuters and township residents, not Silicon Valley investors.
- **Visual-first.** The product is cinematic — a hand raised on a dusty road, a minibus slowing down, a door opening. Show it.

## Platform Strategy

---

### TikTok — Primary Growth Engine

TikTok is where this campaign goes viral or doesn't. The format is perfect: short, visual, emotional, shareable.

**Content pillars:**

1. **"Just raise your hand" demo clips**
   Short POV videos of the hand signal being detected and the minibus slowing down. No voiceover needed. Text overlay: *"No app. No cash. Just your hand."*

2. **Behind the scenes**
   The RB5 unit being installed. The camera feed. The confidence score ticking up on a laptop screen. People love seeing how things work.

3. **Commuter stories**
   Real people. "I spend 2 hours a day waiting for a taxi that may or may not stop." Let the problem sell the solution.

4. **"Did you know?" education series**
   Fast-cut stats about SA's taxi industry. 15 million daily passengers. R90 billion industry. Zero digital infrastructure. Then: *"We're changing that."*

5. **Founder/team content**
   Authentic, unpolished. Building in public. "Here's what we got wrong this week and how we fixed it."

**Posting cadence:** 5–7 times per week during campaign. Engage every comment in the first hour.

**Hashtags:** #TakeMe #SouthAfrica #AutonomousVehicles #AfricanTech #MinibusTaxi #JustRaiseYourHand #TechForGood #BuiltInAfrica

---

### Instagram — Visual Brand Building

Instagram is the brand's portfolio. Every post should look like it belongs in a campaign.

**Content mix:**
- 40% product visuals (the vehicle, the app UI, the RB5 hardware)
- 30% people (commuters, team, taxi operators)
- 20% data/infographics (market stats, safety metrics, privacy architecture)
- 10% milestones (pilot launch, first trip, funding targets hit)

**Reels:** Repurpose best-performing TikToks. Reels get 3× the reach of static posts.

**Stories:** Daily during campaign. Polls ("Would you use a driverless taxi?"), countdown timers to funding milestones, Q&A stickers.

**Highlights:** Keep permanent highlights for: How It Works / Safety / Privacy / The Team / Press

---

### Facebook — Community and Operators

Facebook is where taxi operators, associations, and older commuters live in South Africa.

**Groups to target:**
- SA taxi industry groups
- Township community groups
- South African tech and startup groups
- African innovation and entrepreneurship groups

**Content:** Longer-form posts explaining the operator value proposition. Safety stats. Revenue upside. Fleet visibility. Testimonials from pilot operators.

**Facebook Ads:** Geo-targeted to Johannesburg, Cape Town, Durban. Lookalike audiences based on page engagers. Retarget crowdfunding page visitors.

---

### WhatsApp — Grassroots Distribution

WhatsApp is the most-used app in South Africa. It's not a broadcast channel — it's a word-of-mouth amplifier.

**Strategy:**
- Create a WhatsApp Channel for campaign updates
- Produce shareable content specifically formatted for WhatsApp (vertical video, voice notes from the founder, short text updates)
- Equip early supporters with a share kit: pre-written message + link + short video they can forward to their groups
- Partner with community influencers who run large WhatsApp groups

---

### X (Twitter/X) — Tech and Press Credibility

X is where the tech community, journalists, and investors watch for signals.

**Content:**
- Thread: "We're building a driverless taxi that responds to a hand signal. Here's how it works." (technical thread, goes deep)
- Live-tweet pilot milestones
- Engage with autonomous vehicle, African tech, and fintech conversations
- Tag relevant journalists, VCs, and tech publications

**Target accounts to engage:** African tech journalists, mobility-focused VCs, Qualcomm developer accounts, Google Maps/Firebase developer relations.

---

### LinkedIn — Investor and Operator Pipeline

LinkedIn is the B2B layer. Taxi fleet operators, municipal transport planners, impact investors, and corporate partners live here.

**Content:**
- Founder posts: personal story, why this problem, what we've built
- Technical credibility posts: "Why we chose SNPE over TFLite for on-device inference"
- Market opportunity posts: data-driven, cite sources
- Partnership announcements
- Press coverage reposts

**Posting cadence:** 3–4 times per week. Long-form founder posts perform best.

## Crowdfunding Strategy

---

### Platform Selection

**Primary: Thundafund** (South Africa's leading crowdfunding platform)
- Local credibility, ZAR-denominated, familiar to SA backers
- Strong track record with tech and social impact campaigns
- Lower fees than international platforms for SA campaigns

**Secondary: Kickstarter or Indiegogo** (international reach)
- Run simultaneously or as a follow-on campaign
- Targets diaspora, international supporters, and global tech community
- USD-denominated — useful for international press coverage

**Supplementary: GoFundMe** (community/grassroots)
- Lower barrier to entry for small donations
- Good for the "support African innovation" emotional narrative

---

### Campaign Structure

**Goal:** Set a realistic but ambitious target. Break it into visible milestones so backers feel momentum.

| Milestone | Amount (ZAR) | Unlocks |
|---|---|---|
| Seed | R500,000 | First 5 RB5 units + pilot route setup |
| Growth | R1,500,000 | 20-vehicle pilot fleet + operator dashboard |
| Scale | R3,000,000 | City-wide expansion + ML model iteration |

**Campaign duration:** 45 days. Research shows 30–45 days is optimal — long enough to build momentum, short enough to maintain urgency.

---

### Reward Tiers

Crowdfunding rewards should feel meaningful, not gimmicky.

| Tier | Amount | Reward |
|---|---|---|
| Supporter | R100 | Name in the app credits + campaign updates |
| Early Rider | R500 | 10 free TakeMe trips when the pilot launches |
| Community Builder | R1,500 | 30 free trips + exclusive backer hoodie |
| Operator Partner | R10,000 | Early access to fleet operator dashboard + 1:1 onboarding call |
| Founding Investor | R50,000 | Equity conversation + named on vehicle wrap |
| Corporate Sponsor | R200,000+ | Logo on vehicles + co-marketing + operator dashboard access |

---

### Pre-Launch (4 Weeks Before)

The campaign is won or lost before it goes live. 30% of your funding target should come in the first 48 hours — that requires pre-launch work.

**Week 1–2:**
- Build an email waitlist via a simple landing page ("Be the first to know when TakeMe launches")
- Seed the story with 3–5 tech journalists and bloggers (embargo until launch day)
- Identify and brief 20–30 "founding backers" who commit to backing on day one
- Create all campaign content in advance (video, photos, copy, reward images)

**Week 3–4:**
- Tease campaign on all social channels ("Something big is coming. Just raise your hand.")
- Release a 60-second campaign trailer on TikTok and Instagram
- Brief taxi operator associations and community leaders
- Set up Facebook and Instagram ad campaigns ready to activate on launch day

---

### Launch Day

- Go live at 9am on a Tuesday or Wednesday (highest engagement days)
- Send email to entire waitlist simultaneously
- Activate all paid social ads
- Post across all platforms with the campaign link
- Founder goes live on TikTok and Instagram for 30 minutes — answer questions, show the product
- Ask founding backers to share immediately

---

### During Campaign

**Week 1:** Momentum. Post daily. Celebrate every milestone publicly. "We hit 10% in 24 hours — here's what that means for the pilot."

**Week 2–3:** Storytelling. Go deeper. Commuter stories. Team stories. Technical explainers. Keep the feed alive.

**Week 4:** Urgency. "10 days left." "We're 70% of the way there." Countdown content. Last-chance messaging to people who engaged but didn't back.

**Final 48 hours:** Full push. Email the entire list. Go live again. "We're this close."

## Influencer and Partnership Strategy

---

### Micro-Influencers (SA-based, 10k–100k followers)

More effective than macro-influencers for this campaign. Look for:
- SA tech and startup creators
- Township lifestyle creators
- Transport and commuter content creators
- African innovation advocates

**Approach:** Give them a genuine story to tell, not a script. Offer free trips on the pilot + a small fee. Authenticity converts better than polish here.

---

### Strategic Partners

| Partner Type | Value |
|---|---|
| Qualcomm Developer Network | Technical credibility, potential co-marketing |
| Google for Startups Africa | Visibility, potential grant funding |
| Peach Payments | Co-marketing on the payment story |
| SA taxi associations | Grassroots distribution, operator credibility |
| University transport research departments | Academic credibility, press coverage |
| Municipal transport departments | Policy credibility, pilot route access |

---

### Press Strategy

Target these publication categories in order:

1. **SA tech press** — TechCentral, MyBroadband, Ventureburn — launch day embargo lift
2. **African tech press** — Disrupt Africa, WeeTracker, TechCabal
3. **International tech press** — TechCrunch, The Verge, Wired (pitch the "Africa-first autonomous transport" angle)
4. **Mainstream SA press** — Business Day, Daily Maverick, News24 (safety and social impact angle)
5. **Transport trade press** — Automotive and mobility publications globally

**Press kit contents:** Campaign video, product photos, founder bio, key stats, technical one-pager, privacy/safety explainer.

## The One Thing

If the budget is zero and the team is small, do one thing: **film the product working and post it on TikTok.**

A real minibus. A real road. Someone raises their hand. The vehicle slows down. The door opens. Text overlay: *"No app. No driver. Just your hand. TakeMe."*

That video, posted organically, has the potential to reach millions. Everything else in this document amplifies that moment — but that moment is the campaign.
